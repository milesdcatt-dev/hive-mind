On the H&M marketing site (Desktop/hmjunkland-site-v2), Miles LOVES the solid orange call-to-action
button style ("Text Photos for a Free Quote" — solid #F47A20 orange pill, white text). Use that orange as
the primary accent / attention color.

He DISLIKES "red and green" together. This came up right after I added green→orange gradient top bars +
green borders to the service cards — the forest-green + warm-orange/red reads as clashing / Christmas-y to him.

**Why:** It's his brand and his taste; the orange CTA is the look he wants the eye drawn to. Green-heavy or
red/green accent combos feel off to him even though forest-green is a brand color.

**How to apply:** Lead accents with the ORANGE, not green. Avoid green→orange gradients and green decorative
borders as "pop." Forest green is fine as a base/structural color (text, dark panels, hero) but should NOT be
paired as a contrasting accent against orange/red. When something needs to "jump," reach for the solid orange
first. Confirm with him before reintroducing green as an accent color.
